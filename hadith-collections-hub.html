Authentic Hadith Collections

The Hadith collections represent the second most important source of Islamic knowledge after the Quran. These collections contain the sayings, actions, and approvals of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), carefully preserved and transmitted by generations of scholars.

Each collection has its own unique characteristics, authentication methods, and organizational structure. Scholars have dedicated their lives to preserving these precious teachings and ensuring their authenticity through rigorous chains of narration (isnad).

Sahih al-Bukhari

The most authentic collection of Hadiths, compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Ismail al-Bukhari (810-870 CE).

7,275 Hadiths 9th Century Most Authentic

Sahih Muslim

Second most authentic collection, compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (817-875 CE).

7,500+ Hadiths 9th Century Highly Authentic

Sunan Abu Dawud

Collection focusing on legal Hadiths, compiled by Abu Dawud Sulayman ibn al-Ash'ath (817-889 CE).

5,274 Hadiths 9th Century Legal Focus

Sunan al-Tirmidhi

Comprehensive collection with detailed commentary, compiled by Abu Isa Muhammad ibn Isa (824-892 CE).

3,956 Hadiths 9th Century With Commentary

Sunan al-Nasai

Collection known for its strict authentication criteria, compiled by Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb (829-915 CE).

5,662 Hadiths 9th Century Strict Criteria

Sunan Ibn Majah

Collection that completes the six major Hadith books, compiled by Muhammad ibn Yazid (824-887 CE).

4,341 Hadiths 9th Century Sixth Major

Musnad Ahmad

Largest Hadith collection organized by companions, compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (780-855 CE).

30,000+ Hadiths 9th Century By Companions

Mishkat al-Masabih

Compilation combining multiple collections, compiled by Waliuddin Abu Abdullah (1254-1342 CE).

5,000+ Hadiths 14th Century Combined Sources

Riyadh as-Saliheen

Collection focusing on virtuous behavior and manners, compiled by Imam an-Nawawi (1233-1277 CE).
