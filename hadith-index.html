Major Hadith Collections (Kutub al-Sittah)

The six most authentic and comprehensive Hadith collections that form the foundation of Islamic scholarship and practice.

Sahih Bukhari

Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Ismail al-Bukhari (810-870 CE), this collection is considered the most authentic Hadith compilation with rigorous authentication standards.

Sahih Muslim

Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (817-875 CE), this collection is second only to Sahih Bukhari in authenticity and is highly regarded by scholars.

Sunan Abu Dawud

Compiled by Imam Abu Dawud (817-889 CE), this collection focuses on legal Hadith and is one of the most important sources for Islamic jurisprudence.

Additional Authentic Collections

Other important Hadith collections that complement the major six and provide additional authentic narrations and insights.

Jami at-Tirmidhi

Compiled by Imam Abu Isa Muhammad at-Tirmidhi (824-892 CE), this collection includes Hadith with detailed commentary and grading.

Sunan an-Nasai

Compiled by Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb an-Nasai (829-915 CE), this collection is known for its focus on legal Hadith and rigorous authentication.

Sunan Ibn Majah

Compiled by Imam Muhammad ibn Yazid ibn Majah (824-887 CE), this collection provides additional authentic Hadith not found in other major collections.
