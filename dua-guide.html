Dua (Supplication) Guide

Complete collection of authentic Islamic supplications for daily life, with meanings and proper usage

Understanding Dua

Dua (supplication) is the act of calling upon Allah, asking for His help, guidance, and blessings. It is a direct connection between the believer and Allah, demonstrating our dependence on Him.

Quranic Foundation
وَقَالَ رَبُّكُمُ ادْعُونِي أَسْتَجِبْ لَكُمْ
"And your Lord says: 'Call upon Me; I will respond to you.'" (Quran 40:60)
Prophetic Teaching

The Prophet sallallahu alayhi wa sallam said: "Dua is worship." (Abu Dawud, Tirmidhi)

Daily Morning and Evening Duas

Morning Remembrance (Adhkar as-Sabah)

أَصْبَحْنَا وَأَصْبَحَ الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ
"We have reached the morning and at this very time all sovereignty belongs to Allah. All praise is for Allah."

Evening Remembrance (Adhkar al-Masa)

أَمْسَيْنَا وَأَمْسَى الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ
"We have reached the evening and at this very time all sovereignty belongs to Allah. All praise is for Allah."
When to Recite

Recite morning duas after Fajr prayer until sunrise, and evening duas after Asr prayer until sunset

Ayat al-Kursi (Verse of the Throne)

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ ۚ لَا تَأْخُذُهُ سِنَةٌ وَلَا نَوْمٌ ۚ لَهُ مَا فِي السَّمَاوَاتِ وَمَا فِي الْأَرْضِ ۗ مَن ذَا الَّذِي يَشْفَعُ عِندَهُ إِلَّا بِإِذْنِهِ ۚ يَعْلَمُ مَا بَيْنَ أَيْدِيهِمْ وَمَا خَلْفَهُمْ ۖ وَلَا يُحِيطُونَ بِشَيْءٍ مِّنْ عِلْمِهِ إِلَّا بِمَا شَاءَ ۚ وَسِعَ كُرْسِيُّهُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ ۖ وَلَا يَئُودُهُ حِفْظُهُمَا ۚ وَهُوَ الْعَلِيُّ الْعَظِيمُ
"Allah - there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Self-Sustaining. Neither drowsiness overtakes Him nor sleep. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on the earth. Who is it that can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what is [presently] before them and what will be after them, and they encompass not a thing of His knowledge except for what He wills. His Kursi extends over the heavens and the earth, and their preservation tires Him not. And He is the Most High, the Most Great." (Quran 2:255)
Benefits

Recite in the morning, evening, and before sleeping for protection and blessings

Duas for Daily Activities

Before Eating

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ
"In the name of Allah"

After Eating

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ
"Praise be to Allah"
Prophetic Guidance

The Prophet sallallahu alayhi wa sallam said: "When one of you eats, let him mention the name of Allah."

Entering Home

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ وَلَجْنَا وَبِسْمِ اللَّهِ خَرَجْنَا وَعَلَى اللَّهِ رَبِّنَا تَوَكَّلْنَا
"In the name of Allah we enter, and in the name of Allah we leave, and upon our Lord we place our trust."

Leaving Home

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ وَلَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ
"In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah, and there is no power and no strength except with Allah."

Entering Mosque

اللَّهُمَّ افْتَحْ لِي أَبْوَابَ رَحْمَتِكَ
"O Allah, open for me the gates of Your mercy."

Leaving Mosque

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ
"O Allah, I ask You of Your bounty."

Duas for Protection and Blessings

Protection from Evil

أَعُوذُ بِكَلِمَاتِ اللَّهِ التَّامَّاتِ الَّتِي لَا يُجَاوِزُهُنَّ بَرٌّ وَلَا فَاجِرٌ
"I seek refuge in the perfect words of Allah, which neither the upright nor the corrupt can overcome."
When to Recite

Recite in the morning and evening for protection from harm and evil

Protection from Shaytan

أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
"I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan."

Before Reading Quran

أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيمِ
"I seek refuge in Allah from the accursed Satan."

Seeking Forgiveness

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ لِي وَتُبْ عَلَيَّ إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ التَّوَّابُ الرَّحِيمُ
"My Lord, forgive me and accept my repentance, for You are the Ever-Accepting of repentance, the Most Merciful."

Duas for Specific Situations

When Sneezing

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ
"Praise be to Allah"

Response to Sneezing

يَرْحَمُكَ اللَّهُ
"May Allah have mercy on you"

Response to "Yarhamuk Allah"

يَهْدِيكُمُ اللَّهُ وَيُصْلِحُ بَالَكُمْ
"May Allah guide you and set your affairs right"

When Visiting the Sick

لَا بَأْسَ طَهُورٌ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ
"No harm, it will be purification, if Allah wills."

When Hearing Bad News

إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّا إِلَيْهِ رَاجِعُونَ اللَّهُمَّ أْجُرْنِي فِي مُصِيبَتِي وَأَخْلِفْ لِي خَيْرًا مِنْهَا
"Indeed, we belong to Allah and indeed, to Him we will return. O Allah, reward me in my calamity and replace it with something better."

When Traveling

اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّا نَسْأَلُكَ فِي سَفَرِنَا هَذَا الْبِرَّ وَالتَّقْوَى وَمِنَ الْعَمَلِ مَا تَرْضَى اللَّهُمَّ هَوِّنْ عَلَيْنَا سَفَرَنَا هَذَا وَاطْوِ عَنَّا بُعْدَهُ
"O Allah, we ask You on this journey of ours for righteousness and piety, and for deeds that are pleasing to You. O Allah, make this journey easy for us and fold up for us its distance."

Duas for Spiritual Growth

Increasing Knowledge

رَبِّ زِدْنِي عِلْمًا
"My Lord, increase me in knowledge." (Quran 20:114)

Guidance and Righteousness

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ
"Our Lord, grant us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire." (Quran 2:201)

Patience and Gratitude

رَبَّنَا أَفْرِغْ عَلَيْنَا صَبْرًا وَثَبِّتْ أَقْدَامَنَا وَانصُرْنَا عَلَى الْقَوْمِ الْكَافِرِينَ
"Our Lord, pour upon us patience and plant firmly our feet and give us victory over the disbelieving people." (Quran 2:250)

Seeking Allah's Love

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ حُبَّكَ وَحُبَّ مَنْ يُحِبُّكَ وَالْعَمَلَ الَّذِي يُبَلِّغُنِي حُبَّكَ
"O Allah, I ask You for Your love and the love of those who love You, and for the action that will cause me to attain Your love."

Duas for Family and Relationships

For Parents

رَبِّ ارْحَمْهُمَا كَمَا رَبَّيَانِي صَغِيرًا
"My Lord, have mercy upon them as they brought me up when I was small." (Quran 17:24)

For Children

رَبِّ هَبْ لِي مِن لَّدُنكَ ذُرِّيَّةً طَيِّبَةً ۖ إِنَّكَ سَمِيعُ الدُّعَاءِ
"My Lord, grant me from Yourself a good offspring. Indeed, You are the Hearer of supplication." (Quran 3:38)

For Spouse

رَبَّنَا هَبْ لَنَا مِنْ أَزْوَاجِنَا وَذُرِّيَّاتِنَا قُرَّةَ أَعْيُنٍ وَاجْعَلْنَا لِلْمُتَّقِينَ إِمَامًا
"Our Lord, grant us from among our spouses and offspring comfort to our eyes and make us an example for the righteous." (Quran 25:74)

Etiquette of Making Dua

Proper Manners

  • Face Qiblah: Face the direction of the Kaaba when possible
  • Raise Hands: Raise your hands in supplication
  • Begin with Praise: Start by praising Allah
  • Send Blessings: Send blessings on the Prophet
  • Be Sincere: Make dua with full concentration
  • Be Patient: Don't rush for immediate answers

Best Times for Dua

  • Last Third of Night: Especially during Tahajjud
  • Between Adhan and Iqamah: When calling to prayer
  • During Prostration: While in sujood
  • Friday Afternoon: Special time on Fridays
  • Laylat al-Qadr: Night of Power in Ramadan
  • While Traveling: Duas are more likely accepted

Common Mistakes to Avoid

What to Avoid

  • Rushing: Don't rush through duas
  • Distractions: Avoid making dua while distracted
  • Impatience: Don't give up if not answered immediately
  • Inappropriate Requests: Don't ask for harmful things
  • Lack of Sincerity: Don't make dua without meaning it

Benefits of Making Dua

Spiritual Benefits

  • Connection with Allah: Strengthens relationship with Creator
  • Humility: Reminds us of our dependence on Allah
  • Patience: Develops patience and trust in Allah
  • Gratitude: Increases thankfulness for blessings
  • Protection: Provides spiritual and physical protection

Practical Benefits

  • Stress Relief: Sharing concerns with Allah reduces anxiety
  • Focus: Helps clarify what we truly need
  • Perspective: Reminds us of what's truly important
  • Community: Unites Muslims in common supplications
  • Guidance: Helps us make better decisions
