Five Pillars of Islam

The foundation upon which the entire structure of Islam is built

1
Shahada
Declaration of Faith

The Shahada is the first and most fundamental pillar of Islam. It is the declaration that there is no deity except Allah and that Muhammad is His Messenger. This declaration is what makes a person a Muslim.

It's not just words to be recited, but a profound belief that must be held in the heart and reflected in one's actions. The Shahada is recited during daily prayers, when converting to Islam, and at the end of life.

Key Details

Arabic Text:
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ
I bear witness that there is no deity except Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah.
When to Recite:
Daily prayers, conversion, end of life
Meaning:
Complete submission to Allah and acceptance of Prophet Muhammad's guidance

Why Shahada is Important

  • It's the entry point into Islam
  • Establishes the foundation of monotheism
  • Creates a direct relationship with Allah
  • Unites all Muslims worldwide
  • Provides spiritual identity and purpose
2
Salah
Prayer

Salah is the ritual prayer performed five times daily at prescribed times. It's a direct connection between the worshipper and Allah, serving as a reminder of our purpose in life and our relationship with our Creator.

Prayer purifies the heart, strengthens faith, and provides spiritual discipline. It's performed facing the Kaaba in Mecca and includes specific movements and recitations from the Quran.

Prayer Times & Details

Fajr (Dawn):
2 rakats - Before sunrise
Dhuhr (Noon):
4 rakats - After sun passes zenith
Asr (Afternoon):
4 rakats - Late afternoon
Maghrib (Sunset):
3 rakats - After sunset
Isha (Night):
4 rakats - Night time

Benefits of Salah

  • Maintains spiritual connection with Allah
  • Provides daily structure and discipline
  • Purifies the heart and soul
  • Strengthens community bonds
  • Offers regular moments of reflection
3
Zakat
Charity

Zakat is the obligatory charity that Muslims must give annually if they meet certain wealth criteria. It's typically 2.5% of one's savings and investments. This pillar ensures wealth distribution and helps those in need.

Zakat purifies wealth, teaches generosity, and creates social responsibility. It's not just giving money but also includes giving time, knowledge, and other resources to help others.

Zakat Requirements

Rate:
2.5% of eligible wealth
Nisab (Minimum):
Value of 87.48 grams of gold or 612.36 grams of silver
Frequency:
Annually, after one lunar year
Recipients:
Poor, needy, debtors, travelers, and others specified in Quran

Purpose of Zakat

  • Purifies wealth and soul
  • Reduces economic inequality
  • Strengthens community bonds
  • Teaches gratitude and generosity
  • Fulfills social responsibility
4
Sawm
Fasting

Sawm refers to fasting during the month of Ramadan, from dawn to sunset. Muslims abstain from food, drink, and other physical needs during daylight hours. This pillar teaches self-discipline, empathy, and gratitude.

Fasting is not just about abstaining from food but also from negative thoughts, actions, and speech. It's a time for increased prayer, Quran recitation, and charitable acts.

Fasting Details

Duration:
29-30 days during Ramadan
Daily Period:
From Fajr (dawn) to Maghrib (sunset)
What to Avoid:
Food, drink, smoking, intimate relations
Exceptions:
Children, elderly, sick, pregnant, traveling

Benefits of Fasting

  • Develops self-discipline and willpower
  • Increases empathy for the less fortunate
  • Purifies the soul and strengthens faith
  • Promotes gratitude and appreciation
  • Creates community solidarity
5
Hajj
Pilgrimage

Hajj is the annual pilgrimage to Mecca that every able-bodied Muslim must perform at least once in their lifetime. It takes place during the Islamic month of Dhul Hijjah and involves specific rituals that commemorate the life of Prophet Ibrahim and his family.

Hajj is a profound spiritual journey that unites Muslims from all over the world, regardless of race, nationality, or social status. It's a demonstration of equality and unity in the eyes of Allah.

Hajj Requirements

Frequency:
Once in lifetime (if able)
Timing:
8th-13th of Dhul Hijjah
Requirements:
Physical ability, financial means, safety
Main Rituals:
Tawaf, Sa'i, Arafat, Muzdalifah, Mina

Significance of Hajj

  • Fulfills a major religious obligation
  • Creates global Muslim unity
  • Purifies sins and renews faith
  • Teaches sacrifice and devotion
  • Connects to Islamic history and heritage
