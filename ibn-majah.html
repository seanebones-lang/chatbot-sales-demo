Sunan Ibn Majah

Complete guide to one of the six major hadith collections, known for its comprehensive coverage and additional topics

Collection Information

Sunan Ibn Majah is one of the six major hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah) and is considered the sixth and final major collection. It was compiled by Abu Abdullah Muhammad ibn Yazid ibn Majah al-Rab'i al-Qazwini (d. 273 AH/887 CE) and contains approximately 4,341 hadith.

Compilation Period

Ibn Majah spent many years traveling throughout the Islamic world to collect and verify hadith, visiting scholars in Iraq, Egypt, Syria, and the Hijaz region. He was known for his comprehensive approach and inclusion of additional topics not covered in other major collections.

Methodology and Authenticity

Selection Criteria

Ibn Majah applied rigorous standards in selecting hadith:

  • Chain Verification: Thorough examination of isnad (chain of narrators)
  • Comprehensive Coverage: Inclusion of hadith from various categories
  • Additional Topics: Coverage of subjects not in other collections
  • Authenticity Assessment: Clear indication of hadith reliability
  • Practical Application: Focus on actionable guidance

Grading System

Ibn Majah categorized hadith into different reliability levels:

  • Sahih: Authentic and reliable hadith
  • Hasan: Good hadith with minor weaknesses
  • Daif: Weak hadith with significant issues
  • Munkar: Rejected hadith with major problems
  • Mawdu': Fabricated hadith

He often provided his own grading and commentary on the reliability of narrators and chains.

Famous Hadith from Sunan Ibn Majah

1. The Virtue of Seeking Knowledge

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ مَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَلْتَمِسُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا سَهَّلَ اللَّهُ لَهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Whoever travels a path in search of knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Ibn Majah
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith encourages seeking Islamic knowledge and promises divine assistance in attaining Paradise.

2. Kindness to Parents

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهِ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ رَغِمَ أَنْفُ رَجُلٍ أَدْرَكَ عِنْدَهُ أَبَوَاهُ أَوْ أَحَدُهُمَا ثُمَّ لَمْ يَدْخُلِ الْجَنَّةَ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'May his nose be rubbed in the dust, a man who finds his parents (or one of them) in old age and does not enter Paradise.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Ibn Majah
Hasan (Good)

Significance: This hadith emphasizes the importance of caring for elderly parents and the severe consequences of neglecting them.

3. The Virtue of Good Character

عَنْ أَبِي الدَّرْدَاءِ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ مَا مِنْ شَيْءٍ أَثْقَلُ فِي مِيزَانِ الْمُؤْمِنِ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ مِنْ حُسْنِ الْخُلُقِ
"Abu al-Darda reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Nothing is heavier on the believer's scale on the Day of Resurrection than good character.'"
Narrated by: Abu al-Darda → Ibn Majah
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith highlights the importance of good character and its weight in the Hereafter.

4. The Virtue of Remembrance

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ مَنْ قَرَأَ حَرْفًا مِنْ كِتَابِ اللَّهِ فَلَهُ بِهِ حَسَنَةٌ وَالْحَسَنَةُ بِعَشْرِ أَمْثَالِهَا لَا أَقُولُ الم حَرْفٌ وَلَكِنْ أَلِفٌ حَرْفٌ وَلَامٌ حَرْفٌ وَمِيمٌ حَرْفٌ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Whoever recites a letter from the Book of Allah, he will have a reward for it, and the reward is multiplied by ten. I do not say that Alif-Lam-Mim is one letter, but Alif is a letter, Lam is a letter, and Mim is a letter.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Ibn Majah
Hasan (Good)

Significance: This hadith encourages reading the Quran and explains the great reward for each letter recited.

5. Sincerity in Actions

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَنْظُرُ إِلَى أَجْسَامِكُمْ وَلَا إِلَى صُوَرِكُمْ وَلَكِنْ يَنْظُرُ إِلَى قُلُوبِكُمْ وَأَعْمَالِكُمْ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Allah does not look at your bodies or your appearances, but He looks at your hearts and your actions.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Ibn Majah
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith teaches that Allah values sincerity and good deeds over outward appearance.

Major Categories

Sunan Ibn Majah is organized into several major categories, providing comprehensive coverage:

1. Purification and Prayer

Hadith related to ritual purity and worship:

  • Wudu (ablution) procedures and requirements
  • Ghusl (full bath) and its conditions
  • Five daily prayers and their times
  • Prayer prerequisites and conditions
  • Special prayers and supplications

2. Fasting and Zakat

Guidance on fasting and charity:

  • Ramadan fasting requirements
  • Voluntary fasting and its virtues
  • Zakat calculation and distribution
  • Charity and its benefits
  • Breaking fast and suhoor

3. Marriage and Family

Islamic guidance on family matters:

  • Marriage contracts and conditions
  • Rights and responsibilities of spouses
  • Divorce procedures and regulations
  • Child custody and support
  • Family harmony and communication

4. Business and Transactions

Islamic business ethics and financial matters:

  • Halal and haram business practices
  • Contract validity and conditions
  • Interest-free transactions (riba-free)
  • Partnership and investment guidelines
  • Consumer rights and responsibilities

5. Social Relations and Ethics

Guidance on interpersonal relationships:

  • Neighborly rights and duties
  • Greetings and social etiquette
  • Visiting and hospitality
  • Conflict resolution
  • Community cooperation

Unique Features of Ibn Majah

Additional Topics

Ibn Majah includes topics not covered in other major collections:

  • Medical Traditions: Hadith related to health and medicine
  • Dreams and Interpretation: Guidance on dream meanings
  • Astronomy and Time: Islamic perspective on celestial matters
  • Animal Rights: Treatment of animals in Islam
  • Environmental Ethics: Care for the natural world

Comprehensive Coverage

The collection covers a wide range of Islamic topics:

  • Legal Traditions: Hadith related to Islamic law and practice
  • Ethical Guidance: Moral and character teachings
  • Practical Applications: Real-world implementation of Islamic principles
  • Historical Accounts: Events from Islamic history
  • Contemporary Relevance: Guidance applicable to modern life

Comparison with Other Collections

Sunan Ibn Majah vs. Sahih Bukhari

  • Scope: Ibn Majah covers broader topics, Bukhari focuses on authenticity
  • Additional Content: Ibn Majah includes topics not in Bukhari
  • Organization: Both organize by legal topics
  • Authenticity: Bukhari has stricter authenticity criteria

Sunan Ibn Majah vs. Other Sunan Collections

  • Coverage: Ibn Majah includes more diverse topics
  • Additional Subjects: Unique coverage of medical and scientific topics
  • Practical Focus: Emphasis on daily life applications
  • Modern Relevance: More contemporary applications
