Understanding Islamic Beliefs

Islamic beliefs (Aqeedah) form the foundation of a Muslim's faith. These beliefs are based on the Quran and authentic Sunnah, providing a comprehensive understanding of Allah, His attributes, and the purpose of human existence.

Six Pillars of Iman (Faith)

1. Belief in Allah (Tawheed)

The foundation of Islamic belief is the absolute oneness of Allah. This includes:

Oneness of Lordship (Tawheed ar-Rububiyyah)

  • Creation: Allah alone is the Creator of everything
  • Sustenance: Allah provides for all creation
  • Control: Allah controls all affairs
  • Ownership: Everything belongs to Allah

Oneness of Worship (Tawheed al-Uluhiyyah)

  • Exclusive Worship: Only Allah deserves worship
  • No Intermediaries: Direct connection with Allah
  • Complete Submission: Surrendering to Allah's will

Oneness of Names and Attributes (Tawheed al-Asma wa Sifat)

  • Perfect Attributes: Allah has the most perfect names and attributes
  • No Comparison: Cannot be compared to creation
  • No Negation: Cannot deny His attributes

2. Belief in Angels (Malaikah)

Angels are spiritual beings created by Allah from light who carry out His commands:

Nature of Angels

  • Creation: Made from light (noor)
  • Obedience: Never disobey Allah
  • Invisibility: Cannot be seen by humans normally
  • No Gender: Neither male nor female
  • No Free Will: Always follow Allah's commands

Famous Angels and Their Duties

  • Jibreel: Brings revelation to prophets
  • Mikail: Manages rain and sustenance
  • Israfil: Will blow the trumpet on Day of Judgment
  • Azrael: Takes souls at death
  • Raqib and Atid: Record good and bad deeds
  • Munkar and Nakir: Question souls in the grave

3. Belief in Divine Books (Kutub)

Allah has revealed divine books to guide humanity:

Major Divine Books

  • Scrolls of Ibrahim: First revelations to Prophet Ibrahim
  • Zabur (Psalms): Revealed to Prophet Dawud
  • Tawrat (Torah): Revealed to Prophet Musa
  • Injil (Gospel): Revealed to Prophet Isa
  • Quran: Final revelation to Prophet Muhammad

Characteristics of Divine Books

  • Divine Origin: All come from Allah
  • Guidance: Provide guidance for humanity
  • Preservation: Quran is perfectly preserved
  • Progressive: Each book built upon previous ones

4. Belief in Messengers (Rusul)

Allah has sent messengers to every nation to guide them:

Nature of Messengers

  • Human Beings: All messengers were human
  • Infallible: Protected from major sins
  • Miracles: Given signs to prove their prophethood
  • Guidance: Teach the same core message

Famous Messengers

  • Adam: First human and first prophet
  • Nuh: Prophet of the flood
  • Ibrahim: Father of monotheism
  • Musa: Prophet of the Torah
  • Isa: Prophet of the Gospel
  • Muhammad: Final messenger

Finality of Prophet Muhammad

Prophet Muhammad sallallahu alayhi wa sallam is the final messenger, and no prophet will come after him. His message is universal and for all time.

5. Belief in the Day of Judgment (Yawm al-Qiyamah)

The Day of Judgment is when all humans will be resurrected and judged:

Events of the Day of Judgment

  • Trumpet Blast: Angel Israfil will blow the trumpet
  • Resurrection: All humans will be raised from graves
  • Assembly: Everyone will gather for judgment
  • Scales: Deeds will be weighed
  • Records: Books of deeds will be opened
  • Bridge: Crossing over Hell to Paradise

Signs of the Day of Judgment

  • Minor Signs: Moral decline, increase in knowledge
  • Major Signs: Coming of Dajjal, descent of Isa
  • Final Signs: Sun rising from west, smoke

6. Belief in Divine Decree (Qadr)

Everything that happens is according to Allah's knowledge and will:

Understanding Qadr

  • Allah's Knowledge: Allah knows everything that will happen
  • Allah's Will: Nothing happens without Allah's permission
  • Allah's Creation: Allah creates everything
  • Human Choice: Humans have free will within Allah's plan

Balancing Qadr and Free Will

  • Predestination: Major events are predetermined
  • Free Choice: Daily decisions are our choice
  • Responsibility: We are accountable for our choices
  • Trust in Allah: Accept what we cannot change

Additional Islamic Beliefs

Belief in the Afterlife

Life after death includes several stages:

Stages of the Afterlife

  • Death: Soul leaves the body
  • Grave: Questioning by Munkar and Nakir
  • Barzakh: Intermediate state between death and resurrection
  • Resurrection: Rising from the grave
  • Judgment: Account of deeds
  • Paradise/Hell: Eternal destination

Belief in the Unseen (Ghaib)

Muslims believe in realities beyond human perception:

  • Jinn: Spiritual beings created from fire
  • Shaytan: Evil jinn who mislead humans
  • Heavenly Realms: Seven heavens and their inhabitants
  • Divine Throne: Allah's magnificent throne
  • Preserved Tablet: Where all events are recorded

Common Misconceptions About Islamic Beliefs

Addressing Misunderstandings

Is Allah a "God" like other religions?

No, Allah is completely unique and unlike any created being. He has no partners, no equals, and no limitations.

Do Muslims worship Muhammad?

No, Muslims only worship Allah. Muhammad sallallahu alayhi wa sallam is respected as the final messenger but never worshipped.

Is everything predetermined?

While major events are predetermined, humans have free will in daily choices and are accountable for their actions.

Do Muslims believe in the Bible?

Muslims believe in the original divine messages but recognize that current versions may have been altered over time.
