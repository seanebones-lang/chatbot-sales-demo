Sahih Muslim - The Second Most Authentic Hadith Collection

Sahih Muslim is the second most authentic collection of hadith after Sahih Bukhari. Compiled by Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (821-875 CE), it contains over 7,000 hadith carefully selected from over 300,000 narrations.

Collection Information

Compiler: Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj al-Naysaburi

Period: 821-875 CE (206-261 AH)

Total Hadith: Over 7,000 (with repetitions)

Unique Hadith: Approximately 3,000

Authenticity Level: Second highest among all hadith collections

Methodology: Rigorous chain of narration verification

Famous Hadith from Sahih Muslim

Hadith 1: The Definition of Islam

Narrated by Umar ibn al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him)
أَخْبَرَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ ﷺ فِيمَا يَرْوِي عَنْ رَبِّهِ عَزَّ وَجَلَّ أَنَّهُ قَالَ إِذَا أَحَبَّ عَبْدِي لِقَائِي أَحْبَبْتُ لِقَاءَهُ وَإِذَا كَرِهَ لِقَائِي كَرِهْتُ لِقَاءَهُ
"The Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him) narrated from his Lord, the Mighty and Majestic: 'When My servant loves meeting Me, I love meeting him. When My servant hates meeting Me, I hate meeting him.'"
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith qudsi (divine hadith) teaches that Allah's love for His servant is directly proportional to the servant's love for meeting Him. It emphasizes the importance of having a positive relationship with Allah.

Hadith 2: The Virtue of Seeking Knowledge

Narrated by Abu Hurairah (may Allah be pleased with him)
مَنْ سَلَكَ طَرِيقًا يَلْتَمِسُ فِيهِ عِلْمًا سَهَّلَ اللَّهُ لَهُ بِهِ طَرِيقًا إِلَى الْجَنَّةِ
"Whoever takes a path in search of knowledge, Allah will make easy for him a path to Paradise."
Sahih (Authentic)

Lesson: This hadith encourages seeking Islamic knowledge and promises divine assistance for those who pursue education. It shows that learning is a form of worship that leads to Paradise.

Hadith 3: The Rights of Neighbors

Narrated by Abu Hurairah (may Allah be pleased with him)
مَا زَالَ جِبْرِيلُ يُوصِينِي بِالْجَارِ حَتَّى ظَنَنْتُ أَنَّهُ سَيُوَرِّثُهُ
"Jibreel kept recommending me to treat the neighbor well until I thought he would make him my heir."
Sahih (Authentic)

Importance: This hadith emphasizes the great importance of treating neighbors well in Islam. The repetition of this advice by Angel Jibreel shows how seriously Allah takes neighborly rights.

Hadith 4: The Virtue of Smiling

Narrated by Abu Dharr (may Allah be pleased with him)
تَبَسُّمُكَ فِي وَجْهِ أَخِيكَ لَكَ صَدَقَةٌ
"Your smiling in the face of your brother is charity for you."
Sahih (Authentic)

Lesson: This hadith teaches that even simple acts of kindness like smiling are considered acts of charity in Islam. It encourages positive social interactions and spreading happiness.

Hadith 5: The Importance of Intention

Narrated by Umar ibn al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him)
إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ وَإِنَّمَا لِكُلِّ امْرِئٍ مَا نَوَى
"Actions are only by intentions, and every person will have only what they intended."
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This is one of the most important hadith in Islam, teaching that the value of actions depends on the intention behind them. It emphasizes the importance of having pure intentions in all deeds.

Major Categories in Sahih Muslim

  • Book of Faith: Core Islamic beliefs and principles
  • Book of Purification: Ritual cleanliness and ablution
  • Book of Prayer: Detailed prayer instructions and rulings
  • Book of Zakat: Charity and financial obligations
  • Book of Fasting: Ramadan and other fasting practices
  • Book of Hajj: Pilgrimage rituals and requirements
  • Book of Marriage: Family life and marital relations
  • Book of Business: Commercial transactions and ethics
  • Book of Good Manners: Character, etiquette, and social conduct
  • Book of Destiny: Divine decree and human free will
  • Book of Paradise and Hell: Afterlife descriptions and warnings
  • Book of Repentance: Seeking forgiveness and spiritual renewal

Methodology and Authenticity

Imam Muslim's methodology for collecting hadith was extremely rigorous and included:

  • Chain Verification: Every narrator in the chain was thoroughly investigated
  • Memory Assessment: Narrators' memory and reliability were evaluated
  • Character Investigation: Personal character and piety were considered
  • Cross-Reference: Hadith were verified against multiple sources
  • Meeting Verification: Confirmed that narrators actually met their teachers
  • Time Period Verification: Ensured narrators lived in the same time period
  • Geographic Verification: Confirmed narrators' locations and travel patterns

This rigorous methodology earned Sahih Muslim the reputation of being the second most authentic book after the Quran. Scholars consider it nearly equal to Sahih Bukhari in authenticity.

Comparison with Sahih Bukhari

While both collections are considered the most authentic, they have some differences:

  • Organization: Muslim organized by topic, Bukhari by chain of narration
  • Repetition: Muslim includes more repetitions of the same hadith
  • Length: Muslim's hadith are often longer and more detailed
  • Focus: Muslim emphasizes practical application, Bukhari emphasizes authenticity
  • Acceptance: Both are universally accepted by all Islamic schools

  • Scholarly Recognition: Universally accepted by all Islamic schools
