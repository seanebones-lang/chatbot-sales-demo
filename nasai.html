Sunan Nasai

Complete guide to one of the six major hadith collections, known for its strict authenticity criteria and comprehensive coverage

Collection Information

Sunan Nasai, also known as Al-Sunan al-Sughra (The Small Sunan), is one of the six major hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah) and is considered one of the most authentic collections. It was compiled by Abu Abd al-Rahman Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb ibn Ali ibn Sinan ibn Bahr ibn Dinar al-Nasai (d. 303 AH/915 CE) and contains approximately 5,662 hadith.

Compilation Period

Nasai spent many years traveling throughout the Islamic world to collect and verify hadith, visiting scholars in Iraq, Egypt, Syria, and the Hijaz region. He was known for his extremely strict criteria in accepting hadith and his focus on authentic traditions.

Methodology and Authenticity

Selection Criteria

Nasai applied extremely rigorous standards in selecting hadith:

  • Strict Chain Verification: Thorough examination of isnad (chain of narrators)
  • Authenticity Focus: Emphasis on sahih (authentic) hadith
  • Narrator Reliability: Strict assessment of narrator credibility
  • Cross-Referencing: Verification with other collections
  • Quality Over Quantity: Preferring fewer, high-quality hadith

Grading System

Nasai categorized hadith into different reliability levels:

  • Sahih: Authentic and reliable hadith
  • Hasan: Good hadith with minor weaknesses
  • Daif: Weak hadith with significant issues
  • Munkar: Rejected hadith with major problems
  • Mawdu': Fabricated hadith

He was known for rejecting hadith that other collectors might accept.

Famous Hadith from Sunan Nasai

1. The Virtue of Prayer

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِنَّ أَوَّلَ مَا يُحَاسَبُ بِهِ الْعَبْدُ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ مِنْ عَمَلِهِ صَلَاتُهُ فَإِنْ صَلُّحَتْ فَقَدْ أَفْلَحَ وَأَنْجَحَ وَإِنْ فَسَدَتْ فَقَدْ خَابَ وَخَسِرَ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'The first thing for which a person will be held accountable on the Day of Resurrection is his prayer. If it is sound, he will have succeeded and prospered. If it is defective, he will have failed and lost.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Nasai
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith emphasizes the importance of prayer and its central role in a person's accountability on the Day of Judgment.

2. Kindness to Parents

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ لَا يَجْزِي وَلَدٌ وَالِدَهُ إِلَّا أَنْ يَجِدَهُ مَمْلُوكًا فَيَشْتَرِيَهُ فَيُعْتِقَهُ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'A child cannot repay his parent unless he finds him as a slave and then buys him and sets him free.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Nasai
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith illustrates the immense debt children owe to their parents and the difficulty of fully repaying them.

3. The Virtue of Knowledge

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ مَنْ يُرِدِ اللَّهُ بِهِ خَيْرًا يُفَقِّهْهُ فِي الدِّينِ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Whoever Allah intends good for, He gives him understanding of the religion.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Nasai
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith indicates that understanding Islamic knowledge is a sign of Allah's favor and guidance.

4. Good Character

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِنَّ مِنْ أَكْمَلِ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ إِيمَانًا أَحْسَنُهُمْ خُلُقًا
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Among the believers with the most complete faith are those with the best character.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Nasai
Hasan (Good)

Significance: This hadith connects faith with good character, showing that true faith manifests in excellent behavior.

5. Sincerity in Actions

عَنْ أَبِي هُرَيْرَةَ قَالَ قَالَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يَنْظُرُ إِلَى أَجْسَامِكُمْ وَلَا إِلَى صُوَرِكُمْ وَلَكِنْ يَنْظُرُ إِلَى قُلُوبِكُمْ وَأَعْمَالِكُمْ
"Abu Hurairah reported: The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said: 'Allah does not look at your bodies or your appearances, but He looks at your hearts and your actions.'"
Narrated by: Abu Hurairah → Nasai
Sahih (Authentic)

Significance: This hadith teaches that Allah values sincerity and good deeds over outward appearance.

Major Categories

Sunan Nasai is organized into several major categories, focusing on authentic traditions:

1. Purification (Taharah)

Hadith related to ritual purity and cleanliness:

  • Wudu (ablution) procedures and requirements
  • Ghusl (full bath) and its conditions
  • Tayammum (dry ablution) when water is unavailable
  • Purification from various impurities
  • Cleanliness in prayer and worship

2. Prayer (Salah)

Comprehensive coverage of prayer-related topics:

  • Five daily prayers and their times
  • Prayer prerequisites and conditions
  • Prayer movements and recitations
  • Congregational prayer and leadership
  • Friday prayer and special prayers

3. Fasting (Sawm)

Guidance on fasting and Ramadan:

  • Ramadan fasting requirements
  • Voluntary fasting and its virtues
  • Fasting prohibitions and exceptions
  • Breaking fast and suhoor
  • Laylat al-Qadr (Night of Power)

4. Marriage and Family

Islamic guidance on family matters:

  • Marriage contracts and conditions
  • Rights and responsibilities of spouses
  • Divorce procedures and regulations
  • Child custody and support
  • Family harmony and communication

5. Business and Transactions

Islamic business ethics and financial matters:

  • Halal and haram business practices
  • Contract validity and conditions
  • Interest-free transactions (riba-free)
  • Partnership and investment guidelines
  • Consumer rights and responsibilities

Unique Features of Nasai

Strict Authenticity Standards

Nasai is known for his extremely strict criteria:

  • Narrator Reliability: Only accepts narrators of the highest integrity
  • Chain Verification: Thorough examination of transmission chains
  • Cross-Referencing: Verification with multiple sources
  • Quality Control: Prefers fewer, high-quality hadith
  • Rejection of Weak: Often rejects hadith accepted by others

Comprehensive Coverage

The collection covers essential Islamic topics:

  • Legal Traditions: Hadith related to Islamic law and practice
  • Worship Guidelines: Detailed instructions for religious practices
  • Ethical Teachings: Moral and character guidance
  • Social Relations: Interpersonal relationship guidelines
  • Practical Applications: Real-world implementation of Islamic principles

Comparison with Other Collections

Sunan Nasai vs. Sahih Bukhari

  • Authenticity: Both have very high standards, but Nasai is often stricter
  • Scope: Bukhari covers broader topics, Nasai focuses on essential practices
  • Organization: Bukhari organizes by themes, Nasai by legal topics
  • Commentary: Bukhari includes more explanatory notes

Sunan Nasai vs. Sunan Abu Dawud

  • Standards: Nasai has stricter authenticity criteria
  • Focus: Both focus on legal traditions, but Nasai is more selective
  • Quantity: Abu Dawud includes more hadith, Nasai focuses on quality
  • Acceptance: Nasai is more widely accepted by scholars

Virtues and Benefits

Studying Sunan Nasai

Benefits of studying this collection:

  • High Authenticity: Access to the most reliable hadith
  • Legal Guidance: Clear instructions for Islamic practice
  • Quality Assurance: Confidence in the authenticity of content
  • Comprehensive Coverage: Addresses most aspects of Islamic life
  • Scholar Recognition: Widely accepted by Islamic scholars

Application in Daily Life

How to use these hadith in practice:

  • Worship Practice: Proper performance of religious duties
  • Legal Decisions: Guidance for Islamic legal matters
  • Character Building: Improving personal qualities and behavior
  • Family Relations: Building harmonious family relationships
  • Business Ethics: Conducting affairs according to Islamic principles
