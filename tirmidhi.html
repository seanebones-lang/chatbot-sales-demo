Sunan Tirmidhi

Complete guide to one of the six major hadith collections, known for its comprehensive coverage and detailed commentary

Collection Information

Sunan Tirmidhi, also known as Jami' al-Tirmidhi, is one of the six major hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah) and is considered one of the most comprehensive collections. It was compiled by Abu 'Isa Muhammad ibn 'Isa ibn Saurah ibn Musa ibn al-Dahhak al-Sulami al-Tirmidhi (d. 279 AH/892 CE) and contains approximately 3,956 hadith.

Compilation Period

Tirmidhi spent many years traveling throughout the Islamic world to collect and verify hadith, visiting scholars in Iraq, Khurasan, and the Hijaz region. He was known for his meticulous methodology and comprehensive commentary on each hadith.

Methodology and Authenticity

Selection Criteria

Tirmidhi applied rigorous standards in selecting hadith:

  • Chain Verification: Thorough examination of isnad (chain of narrators)
  • Comprehensive Coverage: Inclusion of hadith from various categories
  • Detailed Commentary: Extensive analysis of each hadith
  • Grading System: Clear indication of hadith reliability
  • Scholar Opinions: Inclusion of various scholarly views

Grading System

Tirmidhi categorized hadith into different reliability levels:

  • Sahih: Authentic and reliable hadith
  • Hasan: Good hadith with minor weaknesses
  • Daif: Weak hadith with significant issues
  • Gharib: Rare or unique hadith
  • Munkar: Rejected hadith with major problems

He often provided detailed commentary on the reliability of narrators and chains.

Major Categories

Sunan Tirmidhi is organized into several major categories, providing comprehensive coverage of Islamic topics:

1. Faith and Belief (Iman)

Hadith related to Islamic beliefs and theology:

  • Articles of faith
  • Belief in Allah and His attributes
  • Belief in angels and divine books
  • Belief in prophets and the Hereafter
  • Divine decree and predestination

2. Purification and Prayer

Comprehensive coverage of worship-related topics:

  • Ritual purity and ablution
  • Five daily prayers
  • Prayer times and conditions
  • Congregational prayer
  • Special prayers and supplications

3. Social Relations

Guidance on interpersonal relationships:

  • Family relations and marriage
  • Neighborly rights and duties
  • Greetings and social etiquette
  • Visiting and hospitality
  • Community cooperation

4. Business and Transactions

Islamic business ethics and financial matters:

  • Halal and haram business practices
  • Contract validity and conditions
  • Interest-free transactions
  • Partnership and investment
  • Consumer rights and responsibilities

5. Character and Morality

Guidance on personal development:

  • Good character and manners
  • Truthfulness and honesty
  • Patience and gratitude
  • Forgiveness and reconciliation
  • Humility and modesty

Unique Features of Tirmidhi

Detailed Commentary

Tirmidhi is known for his extensive commentary on each hadith:

  • Narrator Analysis: Detailed information about narrators
  • Chain Evaluation: Assessment of transmission chains
  • Scholar Opinions: Views of various Islamic scholars
  • Legal Implications: Practical applications of hadith
  • Authenticity Assessment: His own grading of hadith

Comprehensive Coverage

The collection covers a wide range of topics:

  • Legal Traditions: Hadith related to Islamic law
  • Ethical Guidance: Moral and character teachings
  • Historical Accounts: Events from Islamic history
  • Prophetic Biography: Life and teachings of Prophet Muhammad
  • Eschatological Matters: End times and the Hereafter

Comparison with Other Collections

Sunan Tirmidhi vs. Sahih Bukhari

  • Scope: Tirmidhi covers broader topics, Bukhari focuses on authenticity
  • Commentary: Tirmidhi provides more detailed commentary
  • Organization: Tirmidhi is organized by topics, Bukhari by themes
  • Grading: Tirmidhi includes more grading variations

Sunan Tirmidhi vs. Sunan Abu Dawud

  • Focus: Tirmidhi is more comprehensive, Abu Dawud focuses on legal traditions
  • Commentary: Tirmidhi provides more extensive commentary
  • Coverage: Tirmidhi covers more diverse topics
  • Methodology: Tirmidhi includes more scholarly opinions
